My most recent observation is that, I have been recently developing a tendency to leave things unfinished.

For the first couple of months of this year, I would define 7-10 items for an entire month, tracking them on a regular basis. Intially, the tracking was smooth I had 45 days streak going on whilst finishing most of the tasks, but then I started missing days around the end of Februrary due to a small bump in my schedule. This month March, I have hardly tracked 20% of the days.

A list of things I have left open, within the last couple of months:
- Two blogs: one tech + one personal musing.
- Two projects: multilingual transcription with real time translation correction support added and an open source task I spotted.
- Several reading subjects(inconsistent reading pattern, jumping between subjects).

Two fixes I would try to get over this scenario:
- <strong>Would make tracking must again</strong>. Defining a minimum threshold days per week, failing at which makes the weekend painful. During the 45 days streak I was tracking my days, it was a daily visual cue to help me reinforce good behaviour.
- <strong>Shift to "Bucket Tracking" (Fewer, broader goals)</strong>. Instead of listing every specific goal, we would track one goal from each bucket. Specifics can rotate within these buckets. Deviation from the current goal of any bucket would need a proper logical explanation.

<strong>Additional Fix:</strong> Would add month wise tracking data to this blog post as edits, mentioning details. If I add end any other fixes in the above system, would add in these monthly edits as well.

Kudos to the journey I began by publishing this blog in a go! :p
